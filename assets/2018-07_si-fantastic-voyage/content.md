Kobe Bryant's Post-Basketball Ventures (from Sports Illustrated retrospective)

Bryant pivoted to building an entertainment empire focused on young adult content. He displayed the same intensity from his playing days in this new venture, achieving significant success including an Oscar win in 2018.

Granity Studios and Young Adult Fiction:
His larger vision centered on storytelling and creative content. Bryant explained his philosophy: "If Harry Potter and the Olympics had a baby, that would be the world we're trying to communicate."

He created a fictional universe called Granity, described as featuring "fantasy—dreamlike, magical elements—but it's a magic kids can experience." The article notes he discovered this concept during his final NBA training camp in Hawaii, while grappling with the reality of his fading basketball career.

Publishing Ambitions:
Bryant planned to release three young-adult novels written by fantasy-genre authors set within the Granity universe, launching the following year (2019).

Creative Philosophy:
Beyond sports, Bryant sought to develop "storytelling...whether it's mythology or animation, written or film, that can inspire people and give them something tangible they can use in their own lives."
