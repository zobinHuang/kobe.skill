# Kobe Bryant Through the Years: SI Coverage

## Key Phases

### High School Decision (1996)
At 17, Bryant announced he would skip college for the NBA, shocking observers who questioned the move. One critic called it "a total mistake," though Bryant proved doubters wrong.

### Early Stardom (1998-2000)
Magic Johnson predicted Bryant would need "two more years" to master reading the game. By 2000, Bryant balanced team play with individual brilliance, though the question remained whether he could lead a championship without dominating.

### Championship Era (2000-2010)
Bryant won five titles, including early championships alongside Shaquille O'Neal. Observers noted his fearlessness and refusal to be constrained by circumstance. After O'Neal's departure, Bryant captured his fourth title in 2009, proving he could win independently.

### Post-2003 Challenges
Following sexual assault charges that were dropped when accuser declined testimony, Bryant pursued image rehabilitation through on-court excellence rather than media campaigns, letting his play speak louder than interviews.

### Final Act (2016)
Teammate Pau Gasol recalled Bryant's relentless drive: "This is our chance," he said at their first meeting. "Winning time."

### Beyond Basketball
Bryant pivoted to entertainment, winning an Oscar in 2018 and creating fantasy content for young adults — applying the same intensity that defined his 20-year NBA career.
