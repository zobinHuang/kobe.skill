Profile by Lee Jenkins for Sports Illustrated, October 2013.

Bryant is described as "perhaps the last alpha dog, half greyhound and half pit bull." The profile explores the duality of Kobe's competitive nature — his relentless drive paired with deep vulnerability.

Key quotes:

"I have self-doubt. I have insecurity. I have fear of failure. I have nights when I show up at the arena and I'm like, 'My back hurts, my feet hurt, my knees hurt. I don't have it. I just want to chill.' We all have self-doubt. You don't deny it, but you also don't capitulate to it. You embrace it."

The article recounts a pivotal karate lesson at age 4, where young Kobe was knocked down repeatedly but kept getting back up — a formative moment that shaped his mentality.

It describes the period after his Achilles tear as "The Last Chapter." During a playoff loss while injured, Bryant emerged from the locker room to watch from the bench. He received a standing ovation from the Staples Center crowd that moved him to tears — a rare public display of emotion from a player known for his stoic exterior.

The profile paints Bryant as a figure caught between eras: the last of a breed of singular, dominant personalities in the NBA, unwilling to defer or share the spotlight, yet deeply aware of his own mortality as an athlete.
